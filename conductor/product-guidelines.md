# Product Guidelines - Bracket of Death (BOD)

## Prose Style
- **Professional & Concise:** Use clear, direct language. Avoid unnecessary jargon except for established tennis and tournament terminology.
- **Direct & Functional:** Communication should be efficient, especially in administrative interfaces where speed and clarity are paramount.

## Brand Messaging
- **Legacy & Tradition:** Honor the tournament's history (2009â€“Present). The platform should feel like a prestigious, permanent archive of athletic achievement.
- **Reliability:** Messaging should reinforce the accuracy of the scoring and the robustness of the tournament engine.

## Visual Identity & UI Design
- **Evolutionary Design:** Maintain and expand the current visual identity. Core design principles should remain intact while modernizing specific components for better usability.
- **Responsive Consistency:** Preserve the established layout patterns for both desktop and mobile, ensuring a seamless transition between devices.
- **Performance-Driven UI:** Prioritize low-latency updates so that live scoring actions are reflected in the bracket state immediately.

## Layout & Interaction
- **Mobile-Optimized Input:** Interaction targets (buttons, inputs) must be large and touch-friendly for reliable use on courtside mobile devices.
- **Contextual Navigation:** Ensure users can easily navigate between different tournament years, brackets, and personal statistics without losing context.
- **Data Integrity Visuals:** Use clear visual cues to indicate successfully saved states, validation errors, and finalized match results.
